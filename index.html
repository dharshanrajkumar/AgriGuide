Soil Types

Black Soil


Black soil, also known as Regur soil, is rich in clay and holds moisture well. It is ideal for growing cotton, sugarcane, and pulses.

Desert Soil


Dry, sandy soil with low moisture and organic content. Found in arid regions and not ideal for farming without irrigation.

Laterite Soil


Rich in iron and aluminum, reddish in color. Forms in hot, wet climates and supports crops like tea and coffee.

Loamy Soil


A balanced mix of sand, silt, and clay. Fertile and ideal for agriculture due to good drainage and nutrient content.

Red Soil


Rich in iron, giving it a reddish color. Found in warm, temperate regions and suitable for crops like cotton and pulses.

Sandy Soil


Loose and gritty texture with good drainage. Lacks nutrients and water retention, needs organic matter for cultivation.

Chalk Soil


Alkaline and stony, formed from limestone or chalk. Poor in nutrients but can support certain crops like barley and beets.

Volcanic Soil


Formed from volcanic ash and lava. Very fertile and rich in minerals, excellent for growing crops like coffee and fruits.
